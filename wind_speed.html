Summary: Latitude vs. X

Wind Speed in this data set is calculated in miles per hour. The relationship between Latitude and Wind Speed looks inconclusive, but this is expected. According to Why Files, “The wind is simply air in motion, flowing from high atmospheric pressures to low pressures.” This article also goes on to state that “Over the open lakes the wind will be faster than through a stand of trees, where it will be slowed by friction.” These factors indicate wind speed shares more of a relationship with the geography of the city and neighboring region. Simply comparing wind speed to latitude misses these vital detail in discovering trends in wind speed.
